### DISTRICT DATA

**CON Law:** Partial — Tennessee maintains a CON program but is actively dismantling it.
- **2021 (Public Chapter 557):** Health Services and Planning Act overhauled CON, created Health Facilities Commission
- **2024 (HB 2269):** Major exemptions: ASCs no longer require CON; freestanding ERs within 10 miles of parent hospital exempt; burn units, NICUs, open heart surgery, linear accelerators, LTCHs all exempt; closed hospitals in rural/distressed counties can reopen without CON

**Hospital Consolidation — Ballad Health Monopoly:**
- **Ballad Health** formed in 2018 via merger of Mountain States Health Alliance and Wellmont Health System under a state-sanctioned Certificate of Public Advantage (COPA)
- Controls **20 hospitals** serving ~1.1 million residents across 29 counties (TN/VA/KY/NC)
- **The dominant, essentially sole hospital system in Harshbarger's district**
- ER wait times **tripled** since merger
- Ballad failed **~80% of quality-of-care benchmarks**
- Fell short of charity care obligations by **~$148 million** over four years
- NICU closures, trauma center downgrades, relocation of orthopedic care from Kingsport to Johnson City
- Tennessee lowered Ballad's minimum performance score from 85/100 to 70/100
- **16 Tennessee hospitals closed since 2010** (13 rural)

**Harshbarger on Ballad:** Expressed support for ending the COPA by 2028, stating constituents "want more choices when it comes to health care."
