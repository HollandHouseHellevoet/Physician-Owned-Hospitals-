### DISTRICT DATA

**CON Law:** Yes — Georgia HAS Certificate of Need laws (O.C.G.A. Title 31, Chapter 6, established 1979). Recent reform via **HB 1339** (signed April 19, 2024):
- Removed $10M capital expenditure threshold for clinical services
- Exempted bed increases of 10 beds or 20% (whichever greater) over 3 years for hospitals with 60%+ occupancy
- Extended facility relocation radius from 3 to 5 miles
- Exempted reopening of recently closed rural hospitals
- Special exemption for Morehouse School of Medicine hospital

**Hospital Consolidation — Savannah Duopoly:**

1. **Memorial Health (HCA Healthcare)** — For-profit
   - 604-bed hospital, region's only Level I trauma center
   - **Acquired by HCA Healthcare on February 1, 2018 for $710 million** ($430M debt payoff + $280M capital commitments)
   - Formerly nonprofit; converted to for-profit under HCA
   - Building freestanding ERs in Pooler (Carter's hometown) and Richmond Hill

2. **St. Joseph's/Candler Health System** — Faith-based nonprofit
   - Two anchor hospitals: St. Joseph's (330 beds) and Candler (384 beds)
   - Expanding to Pooler, Bluffton SC, Richmond Hill

**Key observation:** HCA Healthcare, the nation's largest for-profit hospital chain, owns the dominant hospital in Carter's district. Healthcare accounts for ~$2 billion in personal income in the Savannah MSA (8% of regional total).
