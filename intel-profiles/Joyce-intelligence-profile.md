**On Medicare Physician Reimbursement:**
> "My district is mostly served by physicians who treat Medicare patients. And we're up against the wall when it comes to Medicare reimbursement right now."

### DISTRICT DATA

**CON Law:** No — Pennsylvania does NOT have a Certificate of Need law. The state's CON program expired December 18, 1996 and was never reinstated. No state-level barriers to new hospital construction or physician-owned facility development.

**Hospital Consolidation:**
PA-13 covers Adams, Bedford, Blair, Cambria, Franklin, Fulton, Huntingdon, Juniata, Mifflin, Perry counties (plus portions of Cumberland and Somerset).

**UPMC dominance is the defining feature:**
- **UPMC Altoona** — 380-bed acute care hospital, regional referral center, **largest employer in Blair County** (~3,000 employees)
- UPMC holds **89% market share** in the Altoona CBSA
- UPMC affiliated with Altoona Regional Health System in July 2013, with a $250M/10-year capital commitment
- Before UPMC, the market had already consolidated: Altoona Hospital merged with Bon Secours-Holy Family Hospital in 2004; Bon Secours campus closed 2012
- **Statewide:** 32 hospitals have closed in Pennsylvania since 2004; another 25 have shuttered core services. 90% of closures were preceded by a merger or acquisition.
