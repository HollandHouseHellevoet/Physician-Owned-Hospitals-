## OVERVIEW

Together they represent the strongest bloc of healthcare-credentialed legislators with direct influence over Section 6001 repeal.

## STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT

### Strongest Allies for Section 6001 Repeal (ranked)

1. **Rep. Neal Dunn** — Original co-sponsor of BOTH POH bills. E&C Vice Chair. Retiring (no electoral risk). Terminal illness creates urgency. Founded and sold a major urology practice. His district (FL) has the most favorable post-CON-repeal regulatory environment.

2. **Rep. John Joyce** — E&C Vice Chair and O&I Chair. Original co-sponsor of H.R. 2191. 25 years running independent dermatology practice in a UPMC-dominated 89% market share district. Co-chairs the Doctors Caucus with messaging platform.

3. **Rep. Greg Murphy** — Caucus Co-Chair on Ways & Means Health. Confirmed H.R. 977 co-sponsor. Former Chief of Staff at ECU Health AND independent practice president. Most vocal on hospital consolidation harms. Health issues may limit engagement.

4. **Rep. Mariannette Miller-Meeks** — Original co-sponsor of H.R. 4002. Ophthalmologist on E&C Health. Her specialty is heavily targeted for hospital acquisition. No CON law in Iowa.

5. **Rep. Buddy Carter** — H.R. 977 co-sponsor. Former Health Subcommittee Chair. But now focused on Senate race, stepped down from chair, and his primary focus is PBM/340B reform.

### Key Swing Vote

6. **Rep. Diana Harshbarger** — Vice Chair of E&C Health (gatekeeping power). Has NOT cosponsored any POH bill. Her district is ground zero for hospital monopoly harm (Ballad Health). Her pharmacy-focused legislative portfolio and family business conflicts may explain her silence on POH issues. Converting her would be strategically significant given her subcommittee position.
